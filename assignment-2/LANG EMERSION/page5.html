Virtual Reality
Augmented Reality
Environment
  • Fully immersive, replacing the real world with a simulated one.
  • Partially immersive, enhancing the real world with digital elements.
Immersion
  • Highly Immersive, users experience a completely artificial environment.
  • Less immersive, users perceive a blend of the real and virtual worlds.
Interaction
  • Users interact with the virtual environment through controllers, hand gestures, or voice commands.
  • Users can interact with digital content overlaid on the real world using touch, gestures, or voice.
Devices
  • VR headsets (e.g., Oculus, HTC Vive, PlayStation VR), controllers, haptic feedback devices.
  • Smartphones, tablets, AR glasses (e.g., Microsoft HoloLens, Google Glass).
User experience
  • Offers a deeply engaging and isolated experience.
  • Provides an interactive and connected experience, blending the real and virtual worlds.
Privacy concerns
  • Generally fewer privacy concerns as users are in a controlled, virtual environment.
  • Potential privacy concerns due to the need to capture and process real-world data.
